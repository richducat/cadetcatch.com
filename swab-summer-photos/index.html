Important dates

The Swab Summer 2026 timeline

Dates marked "estimated" are extrapolated from prior years and were not officially announced at publication — confirm through official Academy channels.

Mon, June 29

Day One. Haircuts, gear issue, the class photo, a 15-minute goodbye. The Coast Guard publishes an official Day One photo release the same week.

Weeks 1–4

Training on campus. No visits, no phones. Photos posted online are the only window in — this is when to check sources most often, and when a one-photo search saves the most time.

~Week 4 estimated

Class-flag ceremony at Mystic Seaport — historically the first event families may attend, with swab phone time afterward. Watch official channels for the 2026 date.

~Mid-August estimated

Sea Trials, then Swab Summer graduation. In 2025 graduation fell on August 16. The Alumni Association's summer eNews pages usually carry the specifics first.

Mon, Aug 24

Classes begin, per the Academy's published calendar.

Sep 25–27

Family Weekend (confirmed on the Academy's yearly calendar). You'll take your own photos for a change.

The source map

Where Swab Summer photos actually post

All links go to official or long-standing community pages. Always follow each page or group's posting rules.

01

Official Facebook page

Check daily, weeks 1–4

facebook.com/CoastGuardAcademy is the Academy's most active channel, posting Swab Summer updates and photo sets all summer. Follow and turn on notifications so batches don't slip past.

02

Official Instagram

Check a few times a week

@uscg_academy posts highlights, and captions often credit the volunteer photographers shooting that week — a tip-off about whose page carries the full set.

03

Coast Guard photo releases

Check around milestones

Official imagery goes out through news.uscg.mil and DVIDS — high-resolution, public-release photos, with dedicated galleries for big events like Day One.

04

Pduddy pics

Confirm coverage first

Longtime volunteer photographer Paul Duddy's Facebook page has been the highest-volume Swab Summer source for years — the Academy's own family guidance points parents to it. Check that he's covering your class this summer.

05

The Academy's Flickr archive

Bookmark as an archive

The Academy Flickr holds 36,000+ photos in dated albums, but hasn't shown new albums since 2021. Glance occasionally; don't rely on it for this summer.

06

The Parents Association

Join once, follow alerts

cgaparents.org runs regional chapters, a members-only photo gallery, and an all-class Facebook group where experienced parents flag new photo drops fast.

07

Your class Facebook groups

Check daily — your early-warning system

The official incoming-class group is facebook.com/groups/uscga2030; per-class parent groups follow the "USCGA - Parents - Class of 20XX" pattern. When a gallery posts, someone here knows within the hour.

08

Alumni Association pages

Check weekly from late July

cgaalumni.org publishes "Swab Summer eNews" pages — usually first with Sea Trials and graduation specifics — and hosts galleries at cgaalumni.smugmug.com.

Spotting tips

How to actually spot your swab

Every parent learns it the hard way: after Day One haircuts, they all look alike. These habits help — and CadetCatch does the first pass for you, so you apply them to a shortlist instead of a whole gallery.

Let the app do pass one

Know the company. The class splits into eight companies, and photographers caption by company and event. One letter home telling you the company lets you skip half of every gallery.

Learn the shirt colors. Companies often wear distinct colors during training evolutions. Match the color first, then the face.

Zoom in on name tapes. On uniforms and PT gear, the name tape is the only sure ID. Zoom before deciding it's not them.

Check the backgrounds. Your swab is just as likely the slightly blurry figure in the second rank. Scan the whole frame.

Anchor on what a haircut doesn't change. Glasses, height relative to neighbors, posture, the way they run. You know your kid's walk from three hundred yards — trust that.

Keep track of what you've found. Save where permitted, note album and date. Come graduation, you'll want the whole summer in order.

Community rules

Photo etiquette in the parent community

Ask before reposting

A photo of your swab usually includes six other swabs. Cropping or asking first is the norm, and moderators enforce it.

Follow each group's rules

Class and Parents Association groups are hand-moderated. Read the pinned posts; promotional and off-topic posts get removed.

Thank the photographers

The people shooting thousands of frames in July heat are volunteers. A comment costs nothing and keeps the ecosystem going.

Save them for your swab too

They can't see any of this right now. A curated album of their own summer is one of the best graduation gifts you can hand them.
